# UX Copywriting — Canada

> Canada-specific terminology, spelling, tone, and UI pattern conventions. Read alongside `guidelines/copy.md`, which defines the base EH voice, grammar rules, and violation checklist. Rules here override or extend the base where CA usage differs.
>
> **Scope:** Interface copy for all Canadian product surfaces — payroll, HR, leave, recruitment, benefits, timesheets, onboarding, and employee self-service. Does not cover marketing, email, or long-form content.

---

## Spelling and language

Canadian English aligns closely with British English (the base standard in `copy.md`). Key points:

- **Cheque** — not "check" (US). Used for payment instruments.
- **Labour, colour, centre, honour, favour, behaviour** — follow British/Canadian spelling as per the base rules.
- **Program** — preferred by the Canadian government over "programme" when referring to government schemes (e.g. "the EI program", "the CPP program"). Use "program" for CA-market government references.
- **Licence (noun) / license (verb)** — noun takes a "c"; verb takes an "s". Same as British English.
- **Enrolment** — not "enrollment" (US). Applies to benefits enrolment, training enrolment, etc.
- **Organise, recognise, realise** — "ise" not "ize".

**Bilingual consideration** — CA product surfaces shown to users in Quebec or bilingual contexts: flag copy that may require French translation to the relevant stakeholder. Do not attempt to write French copy unless explicitly asked. Interface strings intended for bilingual rollout should avoid idioms that do not translate cleanly.

## Tone and voice for CA

The core EH brand voice (Cut the BS, Hero the employer, Lift the conversation) applies unchanged. CA-specific guidance:

**Regulatory and compliance copy** — CRA-related messaging (T4 generation, ROE submission, CPP/EI deductions) must prioritise clarity and precision over brand personality. These are high-stakes, legally significant actions. Write plain, direct statements. Do not use humour, puns, or elevated brand voice in compliance flows.

**Plain language for government interactions** — when copy relates to government submissions (ROE, T4, TD1), use the exact terminology that appears on the CRA form or publication. Do not paraphrase government labels.

**Avoid Americanisms** — CA users are sensitive to US-centric language. Watch for:
- "check" instead of "cheque" for payment instruments
- "social security" (US term) — CA equivalents are CPP and EI
- "vacation" alone is fine; avoid "paid time off (PTO)" as the default label — CA legislation uses "vacation" or "vacation leave"
- "benefits" is correct; avoid "perks" as a substitute in formal copy
- US state references in examples — use Canadian provinces

## Payroll terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| CRA | Canada Revenue Agency. Spell out in full on first use; "CRA" on subsequent references. Never "Revenue Canada" (outdated). |
| CPP | Canada Pension Plan. Spell out on first use. |
| EI | Employment Insurance. Spell out on first use. Never "EI insurance" (redundant). |
| paystub | CA term for pay slip/payslip. One word. Not "pay stub" (two words), "payslip" (UK/AU), or "pay slip" (AU). |
| ROE | Record of Employment. Spell out on first use. Never "ROE form" (redundant — an ROE is already a record). |
| SIN | Social Insurance Number. Spell out on first use. Never "SIN number" (redundant) or "SSN" (US term). |
| T4 | Annual tax slip issued by employers. Not "T-4" or "T4 form" (redundant). Plural: T4s. |
| T4A | Statement of Pension, Retirement, Annuity, and Other Income. Spell out on first use. |
| TD1 | Personal Tax Credits Return. Spell out on first use. |
| WCB / WSIB | Workers' Compensation Board (most provinces) / Workplace Safety and Insurance Board (Ontario). Use the correct acronym for the province in context. Spell out on first use. |
| pay period | Noun phrase. Two words. CA common conventions: bi-weekly (every two weeks) or semi-monthly (twice a month) — use the specific term, not "fortnightly". |
| pay run | Two words. Industry term. Not "payrun". |
| direct deposit | Two words. Not "direct-deposit" as a noun. |
| remittance | Used for employer remittances to CRA. Not "payment" in a CRA context. |
| source deductions | CPP, EI, and income tax withheld from employee pay. Use "source deductions" not "withholdings" (US term). |
| pensionable earnings | Earnings subject to CPP contributions. Use the CRA term. |
| insurable earnings | Earnings subject to EI premiums. Use the CRA term. |

## Leave terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| vacation | Standard CA term for paid annual leave entitlement. Not "annual leave" (AU/UK) in CA context, though "annual leave" is acceptable as a generic label. |
| vacation pay | Statutory minimum pay: 4% of gross earnings (2 weeks) or 6% (3 weeks). Label as "vacation pay", not "holiday pay" (UK). |
| statutory holiday | CA term for public holidays. Not "public holiday" (UK/AU) in CA-specific copy. Abbreviated "stat holiday" in informal UI contexts only. |
| stat holiday pay | Pay entitlement for statutory holidays. |
| personal days | Additional leave days beyond statutory minimums. Acceptable CA term. |
| sick leave | Used for illness-related leave. In federally regulated workplaces: 10 days under Canada Labour Code. |
| bereavement leave | Used for leave following a death. Federally regulated: 3–5 days under Canada Labour Code. |
| maternity leave | Used for leave taken by the birth parent. Federally regulated: up to 17 weeks. |
| parental leave | Used for leave taken by any parent (birth or adoptive). Federally regulated: up to 63 weeks (standard) or 40 weeks (extended). |
| compassionate care leave | Leave to care for a gravely ill family member. Federally regulated: up to 28 weeks. |
| leave without pay | Correct CA term. Not "unpaid leave of absence" unless referring to a formal LOA. |
| leave entitlement | Correct CA term for the amount of leave a worker is entitled to. |

**Provincial variation note** — leave entitlements vary by province. Copy in leave-related flows should avoid stating specific day counts unless the product has confirmed the provincial jurisdiction. Use "your entitlement" or "check your province's requirements" where counts cannot be confirmed.

## Benefits terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| group benefits | CA term for employer-sponsored benefits (health, dental, vision, life insurance). Not "perks" in formal copy. |
| benefits enrolment | Two words. "Enrolment" not "enrollment". |
| RRSP | Registered Retirement Savings Plan. Spell out on first use. |
| DPSP | Deferred Profit Sharing Plan. Spell out on first use. |
| group RRSP | Employer-facilitated RRSP. Distinguish from individual RRSP in copy where relevant. |
| TFSA | Tax-Free Savings Account. Spell out on first use. |
| extended health | Common CA label for supplemental medical/health coverage. Not "private health insurance" or "extras" (AU). |
| dental | Acceptable standalone label for dental coverage in a benefits context. |
| EAP | Employee Assistance Program. Spell out on first use. "Program" not "programme". |
| short-term disability | Hyphenated as an adjective. Abbreviated STD in formal/admin copy only. |
| long-term disability | Hyphenated as an adjective. Abbreviated LTD in formal/admin copy only. |
| waiting period | Period before benefits coverage begins. Not "stand-down period" (AU). |
| open enrolment | Annual period when employees can change benefit elections. Two words. "Enrolment" not "enrollment". |

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|

| overtime | One word. CA threshold: generally 40 hours/week or 8 hours/day, but varies by province — avoid stating specific thresholds in UI copy unless jurisdiction-confirmed. |

| stat holiday pay | Applies when employees work on a statutory holiday — ensure copy distinguishes from regular holiday pay. |

## CA-specific UI patterns

**Date format**
- Display: `DD/MM/YYYY` (e.g. 18/06/2026)
- Data / ISO: `YYYY-MM-DD`
- Do not use US format (`MM/DD/YYYY`) on CA surfaces

**Social Insurance Number (SIN)**
- Display format: `XXX-XXX-XXX` (three groups of three, hyphen-separated)
- Field label: "Social Insurance Number (SIN)"
- Never display a full SIN — mask as `XXX-XXX-X##` or equivalent, showing only the last digits where required
- Never label the field "SSN" or "Social Security Number"
- Never request a SIN on a job application — only after offer acceptance

**Province**
- Use Canada Post 2-letter province/territory codes: AB, BC, MB, NB, NL, NS, NT, NU, ON, PE, QC, SK, YT
- Spell out in full on first mention in body copy; abbreviate in form fields and table columns

**Currency**
- Format: `$` prefix, two decimal places (e.g. `$1,234.56`)
- In multi-market contexts where USD and CAD may both appear, disambiguate with the currency code: `CAD $1,234.56` / `USD $1,234.56`
- Do not use "C$" — use `CAD $` with the currency code

**Phone numbers**
- Format: `(XXX) XXX-XXXX` or `XXX-XXX-XXXX`
- Include country code only when necessary: `+1 (XXX) XXX-XXXX`

**Postal codes**
- Format: `A1A 1A1` (letter-number-letter space number-letter-number)
- Label as "Postal code", not "ZIP code" (US)

**Business number**
- CRA-issued business identifier: `XXXXXXXXX RT XXXX`
- Label as "Business number (BN)", not "ABN" (AU) or "EIN" (US)

## Violation checklist — CA additions

Flag any of the following in CA-market copy:

**Payroll and tax**
- "payslip" or "pay slip" used instead of "paystub"
- "SSN" or "Social Security Number" used instead of "SIN" or "Social Insurance Number"
- "ROE form" (redundant)
- "SIN number" (redundant)
- "EI insurance" (redundant)
- "T-4" or "T4 form" (redundant)
- "Revenue Canada" instead of "CRA" or "Canada Revenue Agency"
- "withholdings" instead of "source deductions"
- Unmasked SIN displayed in UI

**Leave**
- "annual leave" used as the primary label where "vacation" is the CA standard
- "public holiday" used where "statutory holiday" is correct
- "holiday pay" used where "stat holiday pay" is correct
- Specific leave day counts stated without confirming provincial jurisdiction

**Benefits**
- "enrollment" instead of "enrolment"
- "private health insurance" or "extras" instead of "extended health" or "group benefits"
- "superannuation" or "super" used in a CA context (use RRSP, CPP, or group RRSP as applicable)

**Recruitment**
- SIN requested during the application stage (only after offer acceptance)
- "CV" used as the default label where "resume" is the CA standard
- "cancelled check" instead of "void cheque"

**Timesheets**
- "penalty rates" instead of "overtime pay"
- Specific overtime hour thresholds stated without confirming provincial jurisdiction

**UI patterns**
- US date format (`MM/DD/YYYY`) on CA surfaces
- "check" used for a payment instrument (correct: "cheque")
- "social security" used in a CA context
- "ZIP code" instead of "postal code"
- "ABN" or "EIN" instead of "business number (BN)"
- Currency displayed without CAD/USD disambiguation in multi-market contexts
- "C$" used instead of `CAD $`
