# UX Copywriting — New Zealand

> New Zealand-specific terminology, spelling, tone, and UI pattern conventions. Read alongside `guidelines/copy.md`, which defines the base EH voice, grammar rules, and violation checklist. Rules here override or extend the base where NZ usage differs.
>
> **Scope:** Interface copy for all New Zealand product surfaces — payroll, HR, leave, recruitment, benefits, timesheets, onboarding, and employee self-service. Does not cover marketing, email, or long-form content.

---

## Spelling and language

New Zealand English aligns closely with British English (the base standard in `copy.md`). Key points:

- **Colour, favour, labour, honour, behaviour** — follow British/NZ spelling.
- **Centre, theatre** — British/NZ spelling.
- **Organise, recognise, realise** — "ise" not "ize".
- **Enrolment** — not "enrollment" (US).
- **Licence (noun) / license (verb)** — noun takes a "c"; verb takes an "s".
- **Programme** — preferred in NZ for most contexts (arts, government, general use). Use "program" only for computing contexts (software programs). Differs from AU, which favours "program" broadly.
- **Payslip** — one word, British/NZ standard. Differs from AU "pay slip" (two words).
- **Cheque** — not "check" for payment instruments.

**Te reo Māori** — NZ product surfaces may include te reo Māori terms, particularly for leave types (e.g. tangihanga for bereavement). Use the correct macron (tohutō) over long vowels: ā, ē, ī, ō, ū. Do not omit macrons. If unsure of the correct spelling, consult a verified NZ source or flag for review. Do not attempt te reo Māori copy without verification.

## Tone and voice for NZ

The core EH brand voice (Cut the BS, Hero the employer, Lift the conversation) applies unchanged. NZ-specific guidance:

**Regulatory and compliance copy** — IRD-related messaging (payday filing, PAYE deductions, KiwiSaver contributions, student loan deductions) must prioritise clarity and precision. These are high-stakes, legally significant actions. Write plain, direct statements. Do not use humour or elevated brand voice in compliance flows.

**Plain language for government interactions** — when copy relates to IRD submissions or Employment New Zealand requirements, use the exact terminology that appears on the relevant form or publication.

**Avoid Americanisms and AU-isms** — NZ users distinguish their context from both US and AU copy. Watch for:
- "vacation" — NZ legislation uses "annual leave"
- "paid time off (PTO)" — not used in NZ; use "annual leave" or "leave"
- "superannuation" as a generic retirement term — NZ uses "KiwiSaver" (not "superannuation") for the national scheme
- "WorkCover" or AU-specific workers' compensation scheme names — NZ uses "ACC"
- "payrun" — always "pay run" (two words)

## Payroll terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| IRD | Inland Revenue Department. Spell out on first use; "IRD" on subsequent references. |
| IRD number | NZ tax identifier issued to individuals and businesses. Format: XX-XXX-XXX or XXX-XXX-XXX (see UI Patterns). Spell out on first use. Never "TFN" (AU), "SIN" (CA), or "SSN" (US). |
| PAYE | Pay As You Earn. NZ income tax deduction system. Spell out on first use. Note: different from UK PAYE system — same acronym but separate legislation. |
| KiwiSaver | NZ retirement savings scheme. One word. Always capitalised as a proper name. Not "super" or "superannuation" in a NZ context. Employer minimum contribution: 3%. |
| KiwiSaver contribution | Employee and employer contributions to KiwiSaver. |
| ESCT | Employer Superannuation Contribution Tax. Spell out on first use. Tax on employer KiwiSaver contributions. Not "superannuation tax". |
| student loan deduction | Deductions for employees with a student loan, administered through payroll. Use "student loan deduction" not "student debt repayment". |
| FBT | Fringe Benefit Tax. Spell out on first use. NZ employer tax on non-cash benefits. |
| ACC levy | Accident Compensation Corporation levy. Deducted from employee earnings. Spell out "Accident Compensation Corporation" on first use. |
| payday filing | IRD requirement to file employment information with each pay run. Two words. |
| employment information | The data submitted to IRD with each pay run under payday filing. Not "payroll report" in this context. |
| payslip | One word. NZ/British standard. Not "pay slip" (AU) or "paystub" (North American). |
| pay run | Two words. Industry term. Not "payrun". |
| NZBN | New Zealand Business Number. Spell out on first use. 13-digit number. |
| withholding tax | Tax withheld at source (used for schedular payments, non-resident contractors). |
| salary sacrifice | Arrangement where employee trades salary for benefits. Not "salary packaging" as the default NZ term. |
| direct credit | NZ term for electronic bank payment to employees. Also acceptable: "direct deposit". |

## Leave terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| annual leave | NZ standard term for paid leave. Minimum 4 weeks under the Holidays Act 2003. Not "vacation". |
| sick leave | NZ term for leave taken for illness. Minimum 10 days per year (from 2022). Note: NZ retains "sick leave" as a standalone category (unlike AU's "personal/carer's leave"). |
| bereavement leave | Leave following a death. Includes tangihanga (Māori funeral) leave. Minimum 3 days for immediate family; 1 day for others. |
| tangihanga leave | Māori funeral leave. A subset of bereavement leave under NZ law. Use the te reo Māori term in NZ-specific copy where contextually appropriate. |
| public holiday | NZ term for gazetted holidays. Not "statutory holiday" (CA term) or "bank holiday" (UK). |
| alternative holiday | A day in lieu for working on a public holiday. Use "alternative holiday" (the Holidays Act term) not "time off in lieu" or "TOIL" in NZ-specific copy. |
| parental leave | Leave for new parents. Includes primary carer leave, partner's leave, and extended leave. |
| primary carer leave | NZ term for the main leave entitlement for the person primarily caring for a new child. Up to 26 weeks paid (government-funded). |
| partner's leave | NZ term for the secondary carer's leave entitlement. Up to 2 weeks unpaid. |
| domestic violence leave | 10 days paid leave for employees experiencing domestic violence. Use the Holidays Act term. |
| leave without pay | LWOP. Correct NZ term. |
| leave entitlement | Correct NZ term for the amount of leave a worker is entitled to under the Holidays Act. |

**Holidays Act note** — leave entitlements in NZ are governed by the Holidays Act 2003. Avoid stating specific day counts in leave flows unless the product has confirmed the applicable entitlement, as the calculation method can be complex (particularly for irregular hours workers).

## Recruitment terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| CV | NZ common term. New Zealand and UK both use "CV" rather than "resume" as the standard. Use "CV" as the default label in NZ surfaces. |
| job advertisement | Formal NZ term. "Job ad" is acceptable in UI copy. |
| offer letter | NZ common term for the employment offer document. |
| employment agreement | NZ-specific: employment contracts are called "employment agreements" under the Employment Relations Act 2000. Use "employment agreement" not "employment contract" in NZ copy. |
| individual employment agreement | IEA. The standard form of employment agreement for individual employees. |
| collective agreement | Agreement negotiated between employer and union. |
| police vetting | NZ term for a criminal record check via the NZ Police vetting service. Not "background check" or "DBS check" (UK). |
| right to work | NZ immigration requirement. Verify that employees have the right to work in New Zealand. |
| IRD number | Should only be collected after an offer is accepted — never during the application stage. |
| bank account details | NZ format for direct credit setup. See UI Patterns for the account number format. |
| onboarding | One word. |
| new hire | Two words as a noun. Hyphenated as an adjective: "new-hire paperwork". |
| IR330 | Tax code declaration form. Spell out on first use: "Tax Code Declaration (IR330)". |

## NZ-specific UI patterns

**Date format**
- Display: `DD/MM/YYYY` (e.g. 18/06/2026)
- Data / ISO: `YYYY-MM-DD`
- Do not use US format (`MM/DD/YYYY`) on NZ surfaces

**IRD number**
- Display format: `XX-XXX-XXX` (8 digits) or `XXX-XXX-XXX` (9 digits)
- Field label: "IRD number"
- Never display an IRD number in full — mask appropriately
- Never request an IRD number during the application stage (only after offer acceptance)
- Never label the field "TFN", "SIN", or "SSN"

**Bank account number**
- NZ format: `XX-XXXX-XXXXXXX-XX` (bank-branch-account-suffix)
- Field label: "Bank account number"
- Displayed with hyphens between each group

**Region**
- NZ has 16 regions. No standard short codes — use full names in forms (e.g. "Auckland", "Wellington", "Canterbury")
- Abbreviate only in space-constrained table columns if needed

**Currency**
- Format: `$` prefix, two decimal places (e.g. `$1,234.56`)
- In multi-market contexts, disambiguate with the currency code: `NZD $1,234.56`
- Do not use "NZ$" — use `NZD $` with the currency code in multi-market contexts

**Phone numbers**
- Mobile: `02X XXX XXXX`
- Landline: `0X XXX XXXX`
- Include country code only when necessary: `+64 2X XXX XXXX` (mobile) or `+64 X XXX XXXX` (landline, drop leading zero)

**Postcodes**
- 4 digits: e.g. `6011`
- Field label: "Postcode", not "ZIP code" (US) or "postal code" (CA)

**NZBN**
- Format: `XXXXXXXXXXXXX` (13 digits, no spaces)
- Field label: "New Zealand Business Number (NZBN)"

## Violation checklist — NZ additions

Flag any of the following in NZ-market copy:

**Payroll and tax**
- "TFN", "Tax File Number", or "SIN" used instead of "IRD number"
- "superannuation" used as the NZ retirement savings term (correct: "KiwiSaver")
- "super" used instead of "KiwiSaver"
- "pay slip" used instead of "payslip" (AU spelling — incorrect for NZ)
- "paystub" used instead of "payslip"
- "payrun" instead of "pay run"
- "WorkCover" or "workers' compensation" used generically (correct: "ACC" or "Accident Compensation")
- "source deductions" (CA term) or "withholdings" (US term) instead of "PAYE deductions"

**Leave**
- "vacation" used as the primary label (correct: "annual leave")
- "statutory holiday" used where "public holiday" is correct
- "time off in lieu" or "TOIL" used where "alternative holiday" is the Holidays Act term
- "personal/carer's leave" used as the primary NZ label (NZ retains "sick leave" as a standalone term)
- "employment contract" used instead of "employment agreement"

**Benefits**
- "enrollment" instead of "enrolment"
- "workers' compensation" used instead of "ACC" in NZ context
- "superannuation" used instead of "KiwiSaver"
- "program" used in non-computing contexts where "programme" is NZ standard

**Recruitment**
- "resume" used as the default label (correct NZ term: "CV")
- IRD number requested during the application stage (only after offer acceptance)
- "employment contract" used instead of "employment agreement"
- "police check" or "DBS check" used instead of "police vetting"

**UI patterns**
- US date format (`MM/DD/YYYY`) on NZ surfaces
- "ZIP code" or "postal code" instead of "postcode"
- "NZ$" used instead of `NZD $` in multi-market contexts
- IRD number displayed in full in the UI
- Macrons omitted from te reo Māori terms (e.g. "tangihanga" displayed without correct spelling)
