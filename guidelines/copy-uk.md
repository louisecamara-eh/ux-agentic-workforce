# UX Copywriting — United Kingdom

> United Kingdom-specific terminology, spelling, tone, and UI pattern conventions. Read alongside `guidelines/copy.md`, which defines the base EH voice, grammar rules, and violation checklist. Rules here supplement the base for UK-specific regulatory, payroll, and employment terminology.
>
> **Scope:** Interface copy for all UK product surfaces — payroll, HR, leave, recruitment, benefits, timesheets, onboarding, and employee self-service. Does not cover marketing, email, or long-form content.

---

## Spelling and language

UK product surfaces follow British English, which is the base standard defined in `copy.md`. The following are key points where UK usage is most likely to be confused with other markets:

- **Payslip** — one word. British English standard. Not "pay slip" (AU) or "paystub" (North American).
- **Programme** — use for most non-computing contexts (e.g. "the pension programme", "the onboarding programme"). Use "program" for software and computing contexts only.
- **Enrolment** — not "enrollment" (US). Applies to pension auto-enrolment, benefits enrolment, etc.
- **Colour, favour, labour, honour, behaviour** — British spelling throughout.
- **Organise, recognise, realise** — "ise" not "ize".
- **Licence (noun) / license (verb)** — noun takes a "c"; verb takes an "s".
- **Cheque** — not "check" for payment instruments.

No additional spelling overrides from the global base are required for UK surfaces.

## Tone and voice for UK

The core EH brand voice (Cut the BS, Hero the employer, Lift the conversation) applies unchanged. UK-specific guidance:

**Regulatory and compliance copy** — HMRC-related messaging (PAYE submissions, RTI filings, P60 generation, pension auto-enrolment) must prioritise clarity and precision. These are high-stakes, legally significant actions. Write plain, direct statements. Do not use humour or elevated brand voice in compliance flows.

**Plain language for government interactions** — when copy relates to HMRC or The Pensions Regulator submissions, use the exact terminology that appears on the relevant publication or guidance. Do not paraphrase government labels.

**Avoid Americanisms** — UK users are sensitive to US-centric language. Watch for:
- "vacation" — UK legislation uses "annual leave" or "holiday"
- "paid time off (PTO)" — not standard in UK; use "annual leave" or "holiday"
- "social security" — not a UK payroll concept; UK uses National Insurance
- "retirement plan" — UK uses "workplace pension" or "pension scheme"
- "résumé" or "resume" — UK uses "CV"
- "ZIP code" — UK uses "postcode"
- US or AU examples — use UK locations and currency

## Payroll terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| HMRC | His Majesty's Revenue and Customs. Spell out on first use; "HMRC" on subsequent references. Note: changed from "Her Majesty's" in September 2022 — do not use the old form. |
| PAYE | Pay As You Earn. UK income tax and NI deduction system operated by employers. Spell out on first use. |
| NI | National Insurance. Spell out on first use. Both employer NI contributions and employee NI contributions are withheld through payroll. |
| NIC | National Insurance Contributions. Spell out on first use. Use "NIC" as the abbreviation; not "NICs" (plural — the abbreviation is already a compound). |
| NINO | National Insurance Number. Spell out on first use. Format: `AB 12 34 56 C`. Never display a full NINO — mask appropriately. Never label the field "SSN" (US) or "SIN" (CA). |
| P45 | Document issued to an employee when their employment ends. States earnings and tax paid in the current tax year. Not "P-45" or "P45 form" (redundant). |
| P60 | Annual summary of earnings and tax deductions issued by the employer after the end of each tax year. Not "P-60". |
| P11D | Annual return of benefits in kind and expenses provided to employees. Not "P-11D". |
| payslip | One word. British English standard. Not "pay slip" (AU) or "paystub" (North American). |
| pay run | Two words. Industry term. Not "payrun". |
| RTI | Real Time Information. HMRC's digital payroll reporting system. Spell out on first use. Employers submit an FPS (Full Payment Submission) each payday. |
| FPS | Full Payment Submission. The RTI file sent to HMRC on or before payday. Spell out on first use. |
| EPS | Employer Payment Summary. RTI submission used to recover statutory payments or report no payments. Spell out on first use. |
| tax code | HMRC-issued code used to calculate how much income tax to deduct. E.g. `1257L`. Use "tax code" not "tax bracket". |
| emergency tax code | Applied when HMRC cannot issue the correct code. |
| National Living Wage | NLW. The minimum hourly rate for workers aged 21 and over. Capitalise as a proper name. Spell out on first use. Not "living wage" (which is a separate voluntary rate set by the Living Wage Foundation). |
| National Minimum Wage | NMW. Minimum rates for workers under 21 and apprentices. Capitalise as a proper name. |
| SSP | Statutory Sick Pay. Spell out on first use. |
| SMP | Statutory Maternity Pay. Spell out on first use. |
| SPP | Statutory Paternity Pay. Spell out on first use. |
| SAP | Statutory Adoption Pay. Spell out on first use. |
| ShPP | Statutory Shared Parental Pay. Spell out on first use. |
| Apprenticeship Levy | Employer levy on wage bills over £3m. Capitalise as a proper name. |
| UTR | Unique Taxpayer Reference. HMRC-issued number for businesses and self-employed individuals. Spell out on first use. |
| company registration number | Companies House registration number. Lower case (not a proper noun). 8-digit number. |
| salary sacrifice | Arrangement where employee reduces salary in exchange for employer-provided benefits (e.g. pension contributions, cycle to work). Two words. |
| net pay | Employee take-home pay after deductions. |
| gross pay | Employee earnings before deductions. |
| deductions | Tax, NI, pension, and other amounts withheld from gross pay. |

## Leave terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| annual leave | Statutory minimum: 28 days including bank holidays (full-time). Use "annual leave" as the primary label. "Holiday" is also widely used colloquially — acceptable in conversational UI copy. |
| holiday | Acceptable UK term for annual leave in conversational contexts. Both "annual leave" and "holiday" are understood; "annual leave" is the more formal/statutory term. |
| holiday pay | Pay for periods of annual leave. Not "vacation pay" (North American). |
| sick leave | UK common term for leave taken for illness. Also "sickness absence" in formal HR contexts. |
| bank holiday | UK term for public holidays. Not "public holiday" as the primary label in UK-specific copy (though "public holiday" is understood). Not "statutory holiday" (CA). |
| bank holiday entitlement | Whether bank holidays are included within the annual leave allowance or given in addition. |
| maternity leave | Up to 52 weeks. First 26 weeks is ordinary maternity leave; remaining 26 weeks is additional maternity leave. |
| paternity leave | Up to 2 weeks (ordinary) following the birth or adoption. |
| shared parental leave | SPL. Allows parents to share up to 50 weeks of leave. Spell out on first use. |
| adoption leave | Leave for employees adopting a child. |
| parental leave | Unpaid leave to care for a child under 18. Up to 4 weeks per year (maximum 18 weeks total). Note: separate from maternity/paternity/shared parental leave. |
| carer's leave | 1 week unpaid per year for employees with caring responsibilities (from April 2024). |
| bereavement leave | No statutory minimum beyond pregnancy loss and stillbirth. Use "bereavement leave" as the general term. |
| jury service leave | Leave for employees serving on a jury. Not "jury duty" (US term). |
| time off in lieu | TOIL. Time off granted in exchange for extra hours worked. |
| unpaid leave | Correct UK term. |
| leave entitlement | Correct UK term for the amount of leave a worker is entitled to. |

## Benefits terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| workplace pension | UK term for employer-sponsored retirement savings. Not "superannuation" (AU/NZ), "KiwiSaver" (NZ), or "401(k)" (US). |
| auto-enrolment | Compulsory pension enrolment for eligible workers. Hyphenated. Not "automatic enrolment" in UI copy (too long) — "auto-enrolment" is the common term. |
| pension scheme | The pension arrangement. Not "pension plan" (US term) or "pension fund" (which refers to the investment vehicle). |
| The Pensions Regulator | TPR. UK regulatory body for workplace pensions. Capitalise as a proper name. Spell out on first use. |
| private medical insurance | PMI. UK term for employer-sponsored health cover. Not "private health insurance" (though understood) or "extended health" (CA term). |
| group life assurance | UK term for employer-sponsored life cover. "Assurance" not "insurance" for life cover in British English (assurance covers events that will happen; insurance covers events that might happen). |
| income protection | Cover for loss of income due to long-term illness or injury. Also "group income protection". |
| critical illness cover | Lump sum payment on diagnosis of a specified serious illness. |
| death in service | Lump sum paid to dependants if an employee dies while employed. |
| EAP | Employee Assistance Programme. Spell out on first use. "Programme" not "program" in UK context. |
| cycle to work scheme | UK government-approved salary sacrifice scheme for bicycles. Lower case. |
| season ticket loan | Interest-free loan for public transport season tickets. |
| Tax-Free Childcare | HMRC government scheme for working parents. Capitalise as a proper name. Replaced the old Childcare Vouchers scheme (closed 2018). |
| P11D benefits | Benefits in kind reported annually on the P11D form. |
| salary sacrifice | Arrangement where employee reduces gross salary in exchange for non-cash benefits. |
| enrolment | "Enrolment" not "enrollment". |

## Recruitment terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| CV | UK standard term. Stands for curriculum vitae. Not "resume" as the default label. |
| job advertisement | Formal UK term. "Job ad" is acceptable in UI copy. |
| offer letter | UK common term. |
| employment contract | UK common term for the written statement of employment particulars. |
| right to work check | Required by UK immigration law before employment starts. Use "right to work check" — not "background check" for this specific obligation. |
| DBS check | Disclosure and Barring Service check. Criminal record check for UK employees. Spell out on first use. Not "background check" or "police check" as the generic UK term. |
| basic DBS | Lowest level DBS check — shows unspent convictions only. |
| standard DBS | Shows spent and unspent convictions and cautions. |
| enhanced DBS | Highest level — includes barred list checks for roles working with children or vulnerable adults. |
| NINO | National Insurance Number. Should only be collected after an offer is accepted — never during the application stage. |
| references | UK common term. Two words. |
| onboarding | One word. |
| new starter | UK common term for a new employee. Preferred over "new hire" in UK copy. |
| new-starter form | Hyphenated as an adjective. |
| Starter Checklist | HMRC form completed by new starters who do not have a P45. Capitalise as a proper name. Not "new employee form". |

## Timesheets and attendance terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| timesheet | One word. |
| contracted hours | Standard agreed working hours. Not "ordinary hours" (AU/NZ term). |
| overtime | Hours worked beyond contracted hours. |
| time off in lieu | TOIL. Time off granted for extra hours worked rather than pay. |
| Working Time Regulations | WTR. UK legislation limiting average working week to 48 hours. Capitalise as a proper name. |
| rest break | Entitlement to a 20-minute break for shifts over 6 hours (WTR). |
| annual leave | Includes bank holidays in the statutory 28-day entitlement (unless contract provides bank holidays in addition). |
| clock in / clock out | Phrasal verbs — two words. Noun: "clock-in time", "clock-out time" (hyphenated as modifier). |
| shift | Standard term for a scheduled work period. |
| bank holiday working | Working on a bank holiday. May attract enhanced pay depending on contract. |

## UK-specific UI patterns

**Date format**
- Display: `DD/MM/YYYY` (e.g. 18/06/2026)
- Data / ISO: `YYYY-MM-DD`
- Do not use US format (`MM/DD/YYYY`) on UK surfaces

**National Insurance Number (NINO)**
- Display format: `AB 12 34 56 C` (two letters, six digits in pairs, one letter)
- Field label: "National Insurance Number (NI number)"
- Never display a full NINO — mask appropriately
- Never request a NINO on a job application — only after offer acceptance
- Never label the field "SSN", "SIN", or "TFN"

**Currency**
- Format: `£` prefix, two decimal places (e.g. `£1,234.56`)
- In multi-market contexts, disambiguate with the currency code: `GBP £1,234.56`
- Do not use "GBP" without the `£` symbol in multi-market UI

**Phone numbers**
- Mobile: `07XXX XXXXXX`
- Landline (London): `020 XXXX XXXX`
- Landline (other): `01XXX XXXXXX` or `01XX XXX XXXX`
- Include country code only when necessary: `+44 7XXX XXXXXX` (mobile, drop leading zero)

**Postcodes**
- Variable format: e.g. `SW1A 2AA`, `M1 1AE`, `B1 1BB`
- Field label: "Postcode", not "ZIP code" (US) or "postal code" (CA)

**Sort code and account number**
- Sort code format: `XX-XX-XX` (6 digits, hyphen-separated)
- Account number: 8 digits
- Field labels: "Sort code" and "Account number"

**Company registration number**
- Format: 8 digits, e.g. `01234567`
- Field label: "Company registration number"

**Tax year**
- UK tax year runs 6 April to 5 April
- Display format: `2025/26` (not `2025–2026`)

## Violation checklist — UK additions

Flag any of the following in UK-market copy:

**Payroll and tax**
- "Her Majesty's Revenue and Customs" — outdated; use "His Majesty's Revenue and Customs" or "HMRC"
- "payslip" spelled as "pay slip" (AU spelling — incorrect for UK)
- "paystub" used instead of "payslip"
- "SSN", "Social Security Number", "SIN", or "TFN" used instead of "NINO" or "National Insurance Number"
- "withholdings" (US) or "source deductions" (CA) used instead of "deductions" or "PAYE deductions"
- "payrun" instead of "pay run"
- "pension plan" used instead of "workplace pension" or "pension scheme"
- "automatic enrolment" used in UI copy instead of "auto-enrolment"

**Leave**
- "vacation" used as the primary label (correct: "annual leave" or "holiday")
- "statutory holiday" or "public holiday" used where "bank holiday" is the UK standard
- "jury duty" used instead of "jury service"
- "vacation pay" used instead of "holiday pay"

**Benefits**
- "enrollment" instead of "enrolment"
- "life insurance" used where "group life assurance" is the UK term
- "private health insurance" as the formal label (preferred: "private medical insurance" in UK)
- "extended health" (CA term) used in a UK context
- "superannuation" or "KiwiSaver" used in a UK context
- "Childcare Vouchers" referenced as an active scheme (closed 2018; replaced by Tax-Free Childcare)
- "program" used in non-computing contexts (correct: "programme")

**Recruitment**
- "resume" used as the default label (correct UK term: "CV")
- NINO requested during the application stage (only after offer acceptance)
- "police check" or "background check" used as the generic UK term (correct: "DBS check")
- "new hire" used as the primary label (preferred UK term: "new starter")
- "employment agreement" used instead of "employment contract"

**Timesheets**
- "ordinary hours" used instead of "contracted hours"
- "penalty rates" (AU term) used instead of overtime descriptions

**UI patterns**
- US date format (`MM/DD/YYYY`) on UK surfaces
- "ZIP code" or "postal code" instead of "postcode"
- NINO displayed in full in the UI
- NINO requested during the application stage
- Currency displayed without GBP disambiguation in multi-market contexts
- "Social Security" used in a UK context
