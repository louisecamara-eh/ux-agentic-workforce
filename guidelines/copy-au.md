# UX Copywriting — Australia

> Australia-specific terminology, spelling, tone, and UI pattern conventions. Read alongside `guidelines/copy.md`, which defines the base EH voice, grammar rules, and violation checklist. Rules here override or extend the base where AU usage differs.
>
> **Scope:** Interface copy for all Australian product surfaces — payroll, HR, leave, recruitment, benefits, timesheets, onboarding, and employee self-service. Does not cover marketing, email, or long-form content.

---

## Spelling and language

Australian English aligns closely with British English (the base standard in `copy.md`). Key points:

- **Colour, favour, labour, honour, behaviour** — follow British/Australian spelling.
- **Centre, theatre** — British/Australian spelling.
- **Organise, recognise, realise** — "ise" not "ize".
- **Enrolment** — not "enrollment" (US). Applies to benefits enrolment, training enrolment, etc.
- **Licence (noun) / license (verb)** — noun takes a "c"; verb takes an "s".
- **Program** — preferred over "programme" in computing and government contexts (e.g. "the JobKeeper program"). Use "program" for AU-market government references; use "programme" only in artistic or formal institutional contexts.
- **Pay slip** — two words in Australian English. Differs from British "payslip" (one word). See the Payroll terminology table.
- **Cheque** — not "check" for payment instruments.

## Tone and voice for AU

The core EH brand voice (Cut the BS, Hero the employer, Lift the conversation) applies unchanged. AU-specific guidance:

**Regulatory and compliance copy** — ATO-related messaging (STP submissions, BAS lodgement, superannuation payments, TFN declarations) must prioritise clarity and precision over brand personality. These are high-stakes, legally significant actions. Write plain, direct statements. Do not use humour, puns, or elevated brand voice in compliance flows.

**Plain language for government interactions** — when copy relates to ATO or Fair Work submissions, use the exact terminology that appears on the relevant form or publication. Do not paraphrase government labels.

**Avoid Americanisms** — AU users are sensitive to US-centric language. Watch for:
- "vacation" as a standalone label — AU legislation uses "annual leave"
- "paid time off (PTO)" — not standard in AU; use "annual leave" or "leave"
- "retirement plan" or "pension" — use "superannuation" (never "super" in formal copy)
- "social security" — AU equivalents are Centrelink payments, not a payroll concept
- "benefits" referring to statutory schemes — use the specific AU term (superannuation, workers' compensation, etc.)
- US state references in examples — use Australian states and territories

## Payroll terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| ATO | Australian Taxation Office. Spell out on first use; "ATO" on subsequent references. |
| ABN | Australian Business Number. Spell out on first use. 11-digit number. |
| ACN | Australian Company Number. Spell out on first use. 9-digit number. |
| BAS | Business Activity Statement. Spell out on first use. |
| ETP | Employment Termination Payment. Spell out on first use. |
| FBT | Fringe Benefits Tax. Spell out on first use. |
| PAYG | Pay As You Go. Spell out on first use. Refers to the ATO withholding system. Not "PAYG tax" — PAYG is the system; "PAYG withholding" is the correct full term. |
| PAYG withholding | Correct AU term for income tax withheld from employee pay. Not "source deductions" (CA term) or "withholdings" (US term). |
| pay slip | AU term for the earnings summary issued each pay period. Two words. Not "payslip" (British English) or "paystub" (North American). |
| pay run | Two words. Industry term. Not "payrun". |
| Superannuation | AU/NZ retirement savings scheme. Spell out in full. Never abbreviated to "super" in formal copy, button labels, or headings. "Super" is acceptable only in conversational body copy where brevity is essential and the term has already been used in full. |
| Superannuation Guarantee | The minimum employer contribution rate set by law. Not "super guarantee" in formal copy. Abbreviated "SG" only in admin/system contexts after first use. |
| STP | Single Touch Payroll. Spell out on first use. ATO digital reporting framework. Not "STP reporting" (redundant). |
| STP Phase 2 | Current version of Single Touch Payroll reporting. |
| TFN | Tax File Number. Spell out on first use. Never "SSN" (US) or "SIN" (CA). |
| TFN declaration | Form employees complete to declare their TFN and tax status. Not "tax form" as the default label in AU context. |
| payroll tax | State and territory-based tax on wages paid by employers. Two words. Different from PAYG withholding — payroll tax is an employer obligation; PAYG withholding is deducted from employees. |
| workers' compensation | AU term for workplace injury insurance. Varies by state. Not "WorkCover" as a generic label — WorkCover is the Victorian scheme name; other states have different names (e.g. icare in NSW, WorkCover Queensland). Use "workers' compensation" generically. |
| ordinary time earnings | Earnings used to calculate superannuation. Use the ATO term. |
| salary sacrifice | Arrangement where employee trades salary for benefits. Two words. Not "salary packaging" as the default — "salary packaging" is acceptable as a synonym in AU, but "salary sacrifice" is the ATO term. |
| direct deposit | Two words. Used for employee bank payments. |
| remittance | Used for employer payments to the ATO. Not "payment" in an ATO context. |

## Leave terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| annual leave | AU standard term for paid leave entitlement. Not "vacation" as the primary label. Minimum 4 weeks under the Fair Work Act. |
| annual leave loading | Additional 17.5% payment on annual leave for applicable awards. Use "leave loading" in UI labels. |
| personal/carer's leave | Combined leave type under the National Employment Standards (NES). Not "sick leave" as the default label — AU legislation uses "personal/carer's leave". "Sick leave" is acceptable in conversational contexts. |
| compassionate leave | Leave for bereavement or serious illness of a family member. |
| community service leave | Leave for jury duty, emergency management activities, etc. |
| long service leave | LSL. Entitlement after extended service with one employer. Varies by state. Spell out on first use. |
| parental leave | Used for leave taken by any parent. Includes maternity, paternity, and adoption-related leave. |
| unpaid parental leave | Entitlement under the NES. Up to 12 months (extendable to 24). |
| Paid Parental Leave | Government-funded scheme administered by Services Australia. Capitalise as a proper name when referring to the government scheme. |
| public holiday | AU term for gazetted holidays. Not "statutory holiday" (CA/UK) or "bank holiday" (UK). |
| public holiday penalty rates | Higher pay rate for working on a public holiday. "Penalty rates" is correct AU terminology. |
| rostered day off | RDO. A scheduled day off built into a work arrangement. Spell out on first use. |
| leave without pay | LWOP. Correct AU term. |
| leave entitlement | Correct AU term for the amount of leave a worker is entitled to. |

**Award and NES note** — leave entitlements may be set by modern awards or enterprise agreements above NES minimums. Copy in leave flows should avoid stating specific day counts unless the system has confirmed the applicable instrument.

## Benefits terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| superannuation fund | The fund that holds employee retirement savings. Not "super fund" in formal copy. |
| default superannuation fund | The fund contributions go to if an employee does not nominate one. |
| stapled super fund | ATO-assigned fund that follows an employee between jobs. Use "stapled super fund" (ATO terminology). |
| salary packaging | Arrangement where an employer provides benefits in lieu of salary. Also "salary sacrifice". Both are acceptable in AU; prefer "salary sacrifice" for ATO-facing copy. |
| novated lease | A vehicle finance arrangement through salary packaging. Two words. |
| FBT | Fringe Benefits Tax. Spell out on first use. An employer tax on non-cash benefits. |
| private health insurance | AU term for supplemental health cover. Not "extended health" (CA term) or "extras" as a standalone label. |
| income protection insurance | Cover for loss of income due to illness or injury. |
| group life insurance | Employer-sponsored life cover. Not "life assurance" (UK term). |
| EAP | Employee Assistance Program. Spell out on first use. "Program" not "programme" in AU context. |
| enrolment | Two syllables. "Enrolment" not "enrollment". Applies to benefits enrolment, super fund enrolment, etc. |

## Timesheets and attendance terminology

| Term | Correct usage |
|------|---------------|
| timesheet | One word. |
| ordinary hours | Standard contracted working hours. Not "regular hours". |
| overtime | Hours worked beyond ordinary hours. Generally at penalty rates. |
| penalty rates | Higher pay rates for overtime, weekends, and public holidays. Correct AU term. Not "overtime pay" as the sole label — penalty rates apply to specific conditions, not just overtime. |
| time and a half | 1.5× ordinary rate. Used for applicable overtime or weekend work. |
| double time | 2× ordinary rate. Used for applicable Sunday or public holiday work. |
| double time and a half | 2.5× ordinary rate. Applicable for some public holiday work under certain awards. |
| clock in / clock out | Phrasal verbs — two words. Noun: "clock-in time", "clock-out time" (hyphenated as modifier). |
| break | Standard term. "Rest break" or "meal break" where specificity is needed. |
| rostered day off | RDO. A day off built into a roster arrangement. |
| shift | Standard term for a scheduled work period. |
| allowance | Additional payment for specific conditions (e.g. travel allowance, tool allowance). Not "add-on" or "extra pay". |

## AU-specific UI patterns

**Date format**
- Display: `DD/MM/YYYY` (e.g. 18/06/2026)
- Data / ISO: `YYYY-MM-DD`
- Do not use US format (`MM/DD/YYYY`) on AU surfaces

**Tax File Number (TFN)**
- Display format: `XXX XXX XXX` (three groups of three, space-separated)
- Field label: "Tax File Number (TFN)"
- Never display a TFN in full — mask as `XXX XXX XX#` or equivalent
- Never request a TFN on a job application — only after offer acceptance

**ABN**
- Display format: `XX XXX XXX XXX` (two, then three groups of three)
- Field label: "Australian Business Number (ABN)"

**State and territory**
- Standard Australia Post 2–3 letter codes: NSW, VIC, QLD, SA, WA, TAS, ACT, NT
- Spell out in full on first mention in body copy; abbreviate in form fields and table columns

**Currency**
- Format: `$` prefix, two decimal places (e.g. `$1,234.56`)
- In multi-market contexts where AUD and other currencies may both appear, disambiguate with the currency code: `AUD $1,234.56`
- Do not use "A$" — use `AUD $` with the currency code in multi-market contexts

**Phone numbers**
- Mobile: `04XX XXX XXX`
- Landline: `(0X) XXXX XXXX`
- Include country code only when necessary: `+61 4XX XXX XXX` (mobile) or `+61 X XXXX XXXX` (landline, drop leading zero)

**Postcodes**
- 4 digits: e.g. `2000`
- Field label: "Postcode", not "ZIP code" (US) or "postal code" (CA)

**BSB**
- Format: `XXX-XXX` (6 digits, hyphen-separated)
- Field label: "BSB"

## Violation checklist — AU additions

Flag any of the following in AU-market copy:

**Payroll and tax**
- "payslip" used instead of "pay slip" (British English — incorrect for AU)
- "paystub" used instead of "pay slip" (North American)
- "SSN", "Social Security Number", or "SIN" used instead of "TFN" or "Tax File Number"
- "super" used in formal copy instead of "superannuation" (informal only)
- "super fund" used instead of "superannuation fund" in formal copy
- "source deductions" used instead of "PAYG withholding"
- "withholdings" used instead of "PAYG withholding"
- "payrun" instead of "pay run"
- "payroll tax" confused with "PAYG withholding" — these are different obligations

**Leave**
- "vacation" used as the primary label where "annual leave" is the AU standard
- "statutory holiday" or "bank holiday" used where "public holiday" is correct
- "sick leave" as the sole label where "personal/carer's leave" is the NES term
- Specific leave day counts stated without confirming applicable award or agreement

**Benefits**
- "enrollment" instead of "enrolment"
- "extended health" used instead of "private health insurance"
- "life assurance" used instead of "group life insurance"
- "super" abbreviated in formal copy or headings

**Recruitment**
- TFN requested during the application stage (only after offer acceptance)
- "background check" used where "police check" is the AU standard
- "payslip" used instead of "pay slip"

**Timesheets**
- "overtime pay" used as the sole label where "penalty rates" is the AU standard
- Weekend or public holiday rates described without using the correct multiplier term

**UI patterns**
- US date format (`MM/DD/YYYY`) on AU surfaces
- "ZIP code" or "postal code" instead of "postcode"
- "A$" used instead of `AUD $` in multi-market contexts
- TFN displayed in full in the UI
- TFN requested during application stage
- "Social Security" or "SIN" used in an AU context
